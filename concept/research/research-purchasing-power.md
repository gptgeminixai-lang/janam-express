SUMMARY:
The "value of the rupee in your birth year" angle is highly buildable: India has continuous CPI data back to 1957-58 (World Bank/OECD series, and RBI/Labour Bureau CPI-IW back to 1946), so a "₹100 in your birth year = ₹X today" multiplier can be computed for any birth year from the late 1950s onward. The multipliers are dramatic and shareable: ₹100 in 1960 is worth about ₹9,600 today (96x), 1970 is 52x, 1980 is 24x, 1990 is 10x, 2000 is 4.5x, and 2010 is 2.4x (World Bank CPI via in2013dollars.com, updated June 2026). The best free computation sources are the World Bank API indicator FP.CPI.TOTL for India (JSON/CSV, no key needed), RBI's DBIE portal (data.rbi.org.in) which publishes the Handbook of Statistics on the Indian Economy with downloadable Excel tables for CPI, gold/silver prices in Mumbai, and USD/INR exchange rates, and Labour Bureau's CPI-IW series for pre-1958 years.

Concrete anchor prices are abundant and jaw-dropping: gold was ₹184/10g in 1970 vs ₹1 lakh+ in 2025 (BankBazaar year-wise table covers 1964-2025), petrol was ₹0.90/litre in 1970 and ₹8.50 in 1989 (PPAC publishes downloadable Delhi RSP data from 2002; Reuters/IOCL data covers 1989-2002), the Maruti 800 launched at ₹47,500 in Dec 1983, the Ambassador cost ₹14,000 in 1958, a government employee's minimum salary was ₹80/month in 1959 and ₹185/month in 1973 (Pay Commission records), a cup of chai was 20 paise in 1972, a Coca-Cola was ~25 paise around 1970, a movie ticket averaged ₹2-2.50 in the 1970s, and a Bombay-Delhi flight was ₹1,500 in 1980. Gaps to note: milk, thali, bicycle, Bata and train-fare prices before the 1990s exist only as anecdotes (Quora/ScoopWhoop-grade), not official series — safest to present those as "nostalgia" items with hedged sourcing, while gold, petrol, salary, postage, exchange rate and CPI multipliers can all be anchored to official/verifiable tables.

## CPI multiplier: ₹100 in birth year → today (the core engine)
From World Bank/OECD CPI for India (site updated June 2026): ₹100 in 1958 = ₹10,253 today (102.5x); 1960 = ₹9,625 (96.3x); 1970 = ₹5,169 (51.7x); 1975 = ₹2,958 (29.6x); 1980 = ₹2,439 (24.4x); 1985 = ₹1,562 (15.6x); 1990 = ₹1,036 (10.4x); 1995 = ₹648 (6.5x); 2000 = ₹448 (4.5x); 2005 = ₹369 (3.7x); 2010 = ₹243 (2.4x). Series starts 1958 — for 1950s births extrapolate with CPI-IW (Labour Bureau, base 1946) or show 1958 as earliest. Cross-check: officialdata.org gives 1980 → 25.3x (avg annual inflation 7.28%), consistent.
SOURCE: https://www.in2013dollars.com/india/inflation (World Bank CPI; also https://www.officialdata.org/india/inflation/1980 and https://www.inflationtool.com/indian-rupee which has monthly data 1957-2026)
WOW: One line — 'the ₹100 note your parents held the day you were born is worth ₹5,169 of today's money' — instantly personalises inflation for a 1970-born user.

## Car prices: Maruti 800 at ₹47,500 (1983) and Ambassador at ₹14,000 (1958)
Maruti 800 launched December 1983 at ₹47,500 ex-showroom (₹52,500 on-road Delhi); first car handed by PM Indira Gandhi to owner Harpal Singh. Hindustan Ambassador cost ~₹14,000 in 1958 and sold for a bit over ₹5 lakh when discontinued in 2014. Ambassador sold 30,000-40,000 units/year in its 1970s-80s prime. Great birth-decade artifact: pre-1983 births get the Ambassador, 1983+ births get the Maruti 800.
SOURCE: https://www.rushlane.com/first-maruti-800-price-rs-47500-12386710.html; https://dziremagazine.com/the-ambassador-car/; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindustan_Ambassador
WOW: 'The car of your birth year cost less than one month of a software engineer's salary today' — ₹47,500 for a brand-new car is unthinkable to younger users.

## Government salary in birth year: Pay Commission minimum wage series
Central govt minimum monthly salary by Pay Commission: 1st CPC (1946) ₹55; 2nd CPC (1959) ₹80; 3rd CPC (1973) ₹185 recommended, govt raised to ₹196; 4th CPC (1986) ₹750; 5th CPC (1996) ₹2,550; 6th CPC (2006) ₹7,000 (entry ₹6,660 = basic ₹4,860 + grade pay ₹1,800); 7th CPC (2016) ₹18,000. This gives a defensible 'a govt employee earned about ₹X/month when you were born' for every birth year by interpolation.

WOW: 'A government clerk's whole monthly salary in your birth year (₹185 in 1973) buys two movie tickets today' — salary-vs-price contrast is the strongest framing device.

## Newspaper and air travel anchors
Times of India slashed its cover price to ₹1.50 in 1994 (₹1 in Bangalore) during the Times-Hindustan Times price war — implying pre-war prices around ₹2+; 1970s-80s newspapers were 25-50 paise (anecdotal). Indian Airlines Bombay-Delhi airfare in 1980: ₹1,500 (when a clerk earned ~₹500/month — flying was 3 months' salary; today the same fare is ~2 days of median formal salary).

## Computation sources: official CPI series and free APIs
(1) World Bank API — indicator FP.CPI.TOTL (CPI index) and FP.CPI.TOTL.ZG (inflation %) for country IND, 1960-present, free JSON/CSV, no key: https://api.worldbank.org/v2/country/IND/indicator/FP.CPI.TOTL?format=json&per_page=100. (2) RBI DBIE https://data.rbi.org.in — Handbook of Statistics on Indian Economy, ~244 tables incl. CPI, WPI, gold/silver, exchange rates, downloadable Excel/CSV. (3) Labour Bureau CPI-IW (labourbureau.gov.in) — industrial workers CPI back-series to 1946, base 2016=100 with linking factors, the only official route to pre-1958 multipliers. (4) MOSPI CPI base 2012=100 from 2011 (mospi.gov.in / esankhyiki.mospi.gov.in has API). (5) inflationcalculator.in has CSV of 2000-2026 MOSPI CPI. Recommended build: hardcode a year→CPI table (1958-2025) from World Bank, compute multiplier = CPI_2025/CPI_birthyear.
SOURCE: https://api.worldbank.org/v2/country/IND/indicator/FP.CPI.TOTL?format=json; https://data.rbi.org.in; https://inflationcalculator.in/historical-cpi-indices/; https://www.in2013dollars.com/india/inflation
WOW: A single static JSON of ~70 CPI values powers the whole 'your birth-year rupee' engine offline — no runtime API dependency needed.

## Gaps and risk notes for the product
No official public series exists for: movie tickets pre-2008, milk pre-2000, thali/chai/Bata shoes/bicycles/train fares by year (Hero Cycles 1980 and Atlas 1970 prices not found in any indexable source; Bata vintage ad prices not found; Railways year-wise fare tables exist only inside Ministry of Railways annual reports / indiastat paywall — dataful.in lists railway fare datasets at https://dataful.in/datasets/?q=railway+fare). Strategy: anchor each birth-year page on the 5 verifiable numbers (CPI multiplier, gold, petrol, USD rate, govt minimum salary) and decorate with clearly-hedged nostalgia items ('people remember paying ~₹3 for a cinema ticket').
